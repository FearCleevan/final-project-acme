Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts

Principles for consistent application of the NextSense design language across surfaces and contexts.

Do — Embrace
  • Use Instrument Serif exclusively for h1 and h2 headlines to maintain editorial authority
  • Apply #7F9EF8 lavender blue to all primary interactive elements (buttons, links, focus states)
  • Maintain minimum 16px padding around interactive elements for comfortable touch targets
  • Pair dark navy sections with white text for maximum contrast and readability
  • Use 100px or 50% border radius for all primary CTAs to signal modern accessibility
  • Apply subtle box shadows (rgba(43, 46, 63, 0.06) 0px 2px 20px 0px) to cards for gentle elevation
  • Include at least 8px internal padding on all buttons; favor 12–16px for primary actions
Don't — Avoid
  • Mix serif fonts outside of h1/h2 headlines; serif in body copy disrupts modern feel
  • Apply lavender or accent colors to structural elements; reserve accents for interactive states
  • Use border-radius below 4px for input fields (insufficient visual definition)
  • Stack buttons without at least 12px vertical spacing; ensure clear visual separation
  • Use dark shadows on dark backgrounds; pair elevation shadows with light surfaces only
  • Apply multiple competing shadows to a single element (one shadow per level only)
  • Reduce padding below 8px on any interactive element; touch targets must remain comfortable
  • Create text color contrast below 4.5:1 for body copy or links; accessibility is non-negotiable
