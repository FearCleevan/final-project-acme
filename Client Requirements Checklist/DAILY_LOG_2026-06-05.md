## What I Accomplished Today (06/05/2026):

**1. Fixed the Sign In / Sign Up Pages**
The login page was previously redirecting users to a Shopify-hosted page (outside the website) before sending them back. This felt unprofessional and broke the experience. Rebuilt the entire sign-in and registration flow so users stay on the Acme website the whole time — they type their email and password directly on our pages, and it just works. No redirects, no Shopify pages, no confusion.

**2. Fixed Checkout Problems Found During Testing**
Ran a full end-to-end order test (adding items to cart → checking out) and found three separate issues, all now fixed:

- **Wrong email showing at checkout** — The checkout page was showing the developer's test email instead of the customer's email. Fixed by properly linking the customer's account to their shopping cart when they log in, so Shopify always knows who is checking out.
- **Only some items making it to checkout** — When adding 4 items quickly, only 2 or 3 were making it through. This was a timing issue — the website was trying to add items before the cart was fully ready. Fixed so all items are properly captured and sent to checkout regardless of how fast they're added.
- **"Something went wrong" after completing an order** — If a customer came back to the site after finishing an order, they'd see an error because the old checkout link was no longer valid. Fixed so the cart automatically clears itself after the customer proceeds to checkout, preventing the error entirely.

**3. Added "My Crate" Tab to the Account Page**
Logged-in customers now have a 4th tab called "My Crate" on their account page. Instead of only seeing it in the slide-out drawer, they can view their full cart — with item images, names, quantities, and prices — right inside their account. There's also an order summary sidebar with a checkout button. If the cart is still loading, the button shows "Preparing…" and becomes clickable once it's ready.

**4. Fixed the Guest Checkout Button Being Stuck**
Customers who weren't logged in (guests) had their "Proceed to Checkout" button permanently greyed out and unclickable even when they had items in their cart. This was a bug — now fixed. Guests can check out normally.

**5. Added "X Sold" Badges to Products**
Products on the website now show how many units have been sold — for example, "4 sold" — similar to how Amazon, Etsy, and eBay display popularity. The count goes up automatically every time an order is marked as "Shipped" in the admin dashboard. It shows as a small green badge on product cards in the catalog and on the product detail page. The admin products list also has a new "Sold" column so Scott can see which items are moving.

**6. Admin Dashboard — Customers Section Now Shows Real Data**
The Customers section in the admin was previously showing made-up placeholder customers. It now pulls real customer data directly from Shopify — real names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, order counts, and total spent. Clicking on a customer shows their full profile and complete order history. The CSV export also works on real data now.

**7. Admin Dashboard — Analytics Now Shows Real Numbers**
The Analytics page was previously showing made-up numbers for everything. It now shows real figures pulled from actual Shopify orders and customer data:
- Real revenue (today, this week, this month — with % change vs. the previous period)
- Real order counts and fulfillment rates
- Top 5 best-selling products by revenue
- Revenue chart based on real order history (last 90 days)
- Real customer stats (total customers, how many have bought more than once, returning customer rate)

*Note: Visitor session counts, device breakdown (mobile vs desktop), and conversion rates still show estimates — getting exact numbers for those requires either a Google Analytics connection or a higher Shopify plan. This will be added once the GA4 setup is complete.*
