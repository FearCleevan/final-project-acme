### 5. Checkout Bug Fixes — 3 Issues Resolved

While reviewing the checkout flow, three issues were found and fixed.

**Bug 1 — Customer's shipping address was getting lost**
- What happened: When a customer filled in their name, address, and contact details on the checkout form and clicked Continue, that information was being silently dropped. By the time they reached the review step, none of it was carried forward.
- Fixed: The shipping information is now properly saved as the customer progresses through checkout. At Step 3 (the review step), customers now see a "Shipping to" summary card showing their full name, address, email, phone number, and any packing notes they added — so they can confirm everything looks right before placing the order.

**Bug 2 — Currency showed USD instead of CAD**
- What happened: The order summary at checkout was labelling the total as "USD" even though Acme Lamp & Sign operates in Canadian dollars.
- Fixed: The label now correctly reads "CAD."

**Bug 3 — No tax line in the order summary**
- What happened: The cost breakdown at checkout showed the subtotal and freight charge, but had no line for taxes — which could have caused customer confusion at the final total.
- Fixed: A "Taxes & duties — Calculated at checkout" line now appears between the freight charge and the total, so customers understand that taxes will be applied at the Shopify-hosted checkout step.

### 6. Mobile Storefront QA — 3 Bugs Fixed

A full review of the storefront on a phone screen was done. Three issues were found and fixed.

**Bug 1 — Category links in the mobile menu weren't filtering**
- What happened: Tapping a category in the slide-out mobile menu (e.g. "Glass Chimneys") took customers to the catalog page but showed all products — the filter wasn't actually applying.
- Fixed: All six category links in the mobile menu are now correctly wired up. Tapping a category shows only the products in that category.

**Bug 2 — Filter pills and product grid were slightly misaligned**
- What happened: On phone screens, the row of filter pills at the top of the catalog page was shifted 8px to the right compared to the product cards below it. A small detail, but noticeable.
- Fixed: Both elements are now aligned to the same left edge on all screen sizes.

**Bug 3 — Hero section was too tall on mobile**
- What happened: The large hero image at the top of the homepage was taking up 90% of the screen height on phones, pushing all the content below it out of view without any scroll hint.
- Fixed: The hero is now 70% of the screen height on phones — enough to look impactful without burying everything else.

### 8. Content Management — Dropped Sanity, Switching to Simple JSON Files

**The decision:** After reviewing the options, Sanity (a third-party content management system) has been dropped from the project plan entirely.

**Why:** Sanity requires Scott to create and manage an external account. It adds complexity and ongoing dependency on an outside service for something that doesn't need it.

**What replaces it:** A set of simple text files stored directly in the project. Blog posts, customer testimonials, the Heritage timeline, and the Our Story copy will all live in plain files that can be updated and pushed live in under a minute — with no logins, no dashboards, and no third-party accounts required.

This unblocks the entire Week 3 content sprint, which was previously waiting on Scott to set up a Sanity account. That blocker no longer exists.
