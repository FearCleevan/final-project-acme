## What Got Done Today

### 1. Prices Now Show in the Right Currency for Each Customer

Before today, every customer on the site saw prices in Canadian dollars — even if they were browsing from the US, UK, or Europe. Not ideal when you're trying to sell internationally.

Now the website automatically detects where a visitor is coming from and switches the currency to match:

| Customer Location | Currency Shown |
|---|---|
| Canada | CA$ Canadian Dollar (default) |
| United States | US$ US Dollar |
| United Kingdom | £ British Pound |
| France, Germany, Netherlands, and other European countries | € Euro |

This happens automatically — the customer doesn't have to do anything. There's also a small flag dropdown in the top navigation bar they can use to switch manually if they want.

**It also works at checkout.** When a US customer enters a US shipping address, Shopify charges them in USD. UK customer gets charged in GBP. This required setting up Shopify's "Markets" feature behind the scenes — tested and confirmed working with real test addresses.

Scott emailed today asking for exactly this. Replied to let him know it was already live and working.

### 2. Google Merchant Center Is Now Set Up

**What is Google Merchant Center?**
It's the tool that gets Acme's products listed on Google Shopping — the product tiles that appear at the top of Google search results when someone searches for something like "vintage oil lamp chimney."

**What was done:**
- Created and configured the Merchant Center account under Acme Vintage Supply
- Linked it to the Shopify store — Google automatically found **10 products**
- Set up shipping rules for all countries:
  - **Canada:** Free shipping, 5–12 days
  - **US, UK, Australia, France, Germany, Netherlands:** CA$30 flat rate, 8–23 days
- Added a **return policy** covering all 7 countries (14-day window, by mail)
- Website (acmevintagesupply.com) was already verified by Google ✅

**Current status:** All 10 products are showing as "Not approved" — but this is completely normal for a brand new Merchant Center account. Google says it takes up to **3 business days** to review new stores. Check back on **July 3** and they should be approved and appearing on Google Shopping.
