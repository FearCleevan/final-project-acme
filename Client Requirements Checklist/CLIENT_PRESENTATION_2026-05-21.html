01.
Prototype

What was built

A fully functional e-commerce storefront prototype built on Next.js 16 — not a mockup, not a wireframe. Every page is live, every user flow works end to end. Three prototype designs were produced; the recommended design was selected based on the client's brief: vintage, premium, non-template.

Note on prototype content: All product names, descriptions, pricing, addresses, photography, and copy visible in the prototype are developer-created placeholders — written to demonstrate the layout and functionality. None of it is final. All content will be replaced with real client-provided information before launch.

02.
Architecture

How the site works — three services, three jobs

The storefront runs on three platforms that each handle a distinct role. Think of it like a physical store: the building, the cash register, and the signage.

The Website · What customers see
Next.js Storefront
Hosted on Vercel · Delivers every page instantly · Handles security & uptime
↓ Transactional data ↓ Editorial content
The Cash Register
Shopify
  • All 50 products & stock levels
  • Cart & checkout
  • Payment processing
  • Customer accounts
  • Orders & shipping
The Signage & Displays
Sanity CMS
  • Journal / Bench Notes articles
  • Heritage timeline
  • Our Story copy
  • Testimonials
  • Editable by you — no developer needed

Sanity is a browser-based content editor. Once live, you can write and publish Journal articles, update the Heritage page, and change site copy yourself — without touching any code, the same way you'd write an email.
