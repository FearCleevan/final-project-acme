## What Got Done Today

### 1. Store Analytics + Admin Activity Log

The store now keeps track of which pages customers visit. Scott can log into the admin and see traffic — which products people are looking at, which pages they land on, and how they move around the site.

On top of that, the admin panel now keeps a log of every action taken inside it — adding products, updating orders, changing content. If something changes, there's now a record of it.

---

### 2. Order Stages Now Save Properly

There are custom order stages that exist before Shopify gets involved — like "Packed at Workshop." These were getting wiped out whenever the page refreshed. That's fixed. The stages now stick even after a reload.

---

### 3. Back-in-Stock Notifications

If a product is out of stock, customers can now leave their email to be notified when it comes back. When Scott restocks something in the admin, an email goes out automatically to everyone who signed up for that product.

---

### 4. Emails Now Come From the Real Domain

Order confirmation and shipping emails were going out from a generic address. They now send from `acmevintagesupply.com`, which looks more professional and is less likely to land in spam.

---

### 5. Order Tracking Page Got a Visual Upgrade

The order tracking page used to show a vertical list of stages. It now shows a clean horizontal progress bar — Order Confirmed → Packed at Workshop → Shipped → Out for Delivery → Delivered — so customers can see at a glance where their order is.

---

### 6. Email Buttons Now Go to the Right Page

Every email Shopify sends to customers (order confirmation, shipping update, etc.) used to have a "View your order" button that sent them to Shopify's generic page. Those buttons now go straight to the Acme tracking page instead.

Better yet, when a customer clicks the link, the page already knows who they are — it pulls up their order automatically. No typing needed.

This was tested live on a real order (ACMEORDER-1026) and it works correctly.
