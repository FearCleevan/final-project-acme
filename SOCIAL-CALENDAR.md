> 🔦 LAMP GUIDE: How to choose the right chimney for your oil lamp
>
> One of the most common questions we get: "Which chimney fits my burner?"
>
> Here's the quick guide:
>
> ✅ **Center-draft burners** (Aladdin, Rayo) → Need a wide, round chimney — typically 4"+ base diameter
> ✅ **Flat-wick burners** (most common antique lamps) → Fit the classic "chimney" shape, measure the base opening diameter
> ✅ **Mantle lamps** → Require specific chimney shapes — always match to manufacturer
>
> The key measurement: **the base opening diameter.** Not the height, not the top — the base ring that sits on the burner.
>
> We make chimneys in borosilicate glass (mouth-blown), which handles the thermal stress of a real flame. Regular soda-lime glass cracks from the heat.
>

> 🏮 A BRIEF HISTORY OF THE OIL LAMP IN NORTH AMERICA (and why the parts are so hard to find)
>
> **1850s–1870s:** Coal oil (kerosene) lamps replace whale oil. An entire industry explodes — hundreds of burner designs, glass chimney manufacturers, shade makers.
>
> **1880s–1900s:** The "golden age." Center-draft burners like the Rayo and the B&H reach peak design. Parlor lamps with etched shades become status symbols.
>
> **1910s–1930s:** Aladdin perfects the mantle lamp. Rural North America still runs on kerosene.
>
> **1940s–1960s:** Rural electrification acts wipe out most of the market. Manufacturers close. Dies are sold off or scrapped.
>
> **Today:** Original parts are scarce. Reproduction quality varies wildly. Most of what you find online is machine-pressed import glass that wasn't made to original spec.
>
> This is why we exist.

**DAY 11 — Thursday**

**Facebook Page:**
- **Type:** Myth-buster — Pillar 1
- **Post:**
> MYTH: "All glass chimneys are basically the same."
>
> REALITY: The glass type, wall thickness, and production method determine whether your chimney lasts one season or thirty years.
>
> Here's what actually matters:
>
> ❌ **Soda-lime glass** (most cheap chimneys): Cracks under thermal shock. One cold draft with a hot chimney = broken glass.
>
> ✅ **Borosilicate glass** (what we use): Same glass as laboratory equipment. Handles rapid temperature change. Made for open-flame use.
>
> ❌ **Machine-pressed glass**: Uneven wall thickness creates stress points. Looks fine until it doesn't.
>
> ✅ **Mouth-blown glass**: Consistent wall thickness throughout. No stress points. How all chimneys were made before industrialization.
>
> The price difference between a $15 chimney and our $60 Miller No. 3 is the difference between replacing it every year and never replacing it.
>
> Any questions about chimney types? Ask below. 👇

> When we talk about "original dies," here's what that actually means.

> A die is a precision tool — hardened steel, machined to exact tolerances — used to press, form, or mold a part to a specific shape. In lamp manufacturing, dies were used for everything: the burner collar, the chimney gallery, the font cap.

> 🔦 HOW TO STORE OIL LAMP GLASS (and stop losing chimneys to breakage)
>
> Collector tip: Most glass loss happens in storage, not in use.
>
> Here's what works:
>
> 1. **Never stack chimneys directly.** Always use bubble wrap or cloth between them.
> 2. **Store vertically**, not horizontally. A chimney stored on its side develops stress points at the contact area over years.
> 3. **Keep away from temperature extremes.** Attic and garage storage cycles the glass through expansion/contraction. A basement is better.
> 4. **Label your glass.** A masking tape label on the base (not the glass) with burner type and source saves hours of matching later.
>
